trails

Fairway Falls Trail

A short, straightforward trail that leads to a flowing waterfall. The waterfall is most active during the winter months, but still manages to drip during the summer.

A dog friendly trail, this hike is very simple and suitable for anyone. Look out for banana slugs and caterpillars.

Little Mountain

Little Mountain is located in Ohair Park, and the trail consists of high elevation and limited shade. Especially hot in the summer.

Make sure to wear sturdy shoes, as some uphill parts are slippery. Once reached the top, there are great views of all of Novato and the surrounding hills.

There is also a farm near the beginning of the trail, so look out for cats and cows.

Pinheiro, Rush Creek, Bahia, and North Levee Loop

A bit of a longer hike, this trail is a part of the Rush Creek Open Space Preserve. One of the many trails that this space offers, this trail takes you through wetlands and into the trees.

There are some points with high elevation, so wear sturdy shoes. This is a good trail for any bird watchers, with a variety of birds to spot in the wetlands.

Look out for deer too, especially during the spring and summer.

Schwindt, Witzel, and Pacheco Pond Tail

One of the many trails at Indian Valley Open Space Preserve, this shorter trail takes you to Pacheco Pond, which has turtles, frogs, and fish.

The rest of the trail is among trees, with small waterways scattered throughout. Be careful of snakes during the summer.

Olompali Trail

Olompali State Historic Park is an old Miwok settlement that has multiple trails within. There are also historical structures and informational plaques throughout the park.

The trail is mainly shaded and within trees. There is an eight dollar day fee to enter, which can be purchased upon arrival via a QR code.

Big Trees and Upper Meadow Trail Loop

This loop trail is a part of the Indian Tree Open Space Preserve. The trailhead is located at the beginning of a long dirt road at the end of Vineyard Drive in Novato.

The trail breaks off into a couple of other routes, also listed on this website. This hike has lots of lush greenery all year round, and also offers a great view of Stafford Lake and hills surrounding. Watch out for poison oak.

Bay Area Ridge Trail

Also a part of the Indian Tree Open Space Preserve, this out and back trail takes a somewhat similar route to the Big Trees trail.

This trail leads up a little higher, offering great views of the town. A good combination of redwoods with shade and bushes with sun, during the summertime especially.

Look out for lizards and banana slugs crossing.

Deer Camp Fire Road

Deer Camp, a looped trail, is the shortest trail among those in the Indian Tree Open Space Preserve. Like the other trails, this one offers great views of the town

and is mainly shaded among trees, with some running water during certain times of the year.
